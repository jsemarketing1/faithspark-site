## What Most Women's Devotional Apps Get Wrong

There's no shortage of devotional apps marketed to women. Most of them have a few things in common:

They're beautifully designed. They use words like "flourish" and "grace" and "beloved." They feel thoughtful and gentle. And they send everyone the exact same reading, every day, regardless of whether you're a mom of four in the middle of the school year, a single woman navigating a career shift, a woman in recovery, or someone sitting with grief.

The devotional might be good. But if it wasn't written for your season, it lands flat. You read it, close the app, and move on — with no sense that anything connected.
