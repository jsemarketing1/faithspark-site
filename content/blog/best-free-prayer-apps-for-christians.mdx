I've looked at a lot of Christian prayer apps over the past few years. Most of them do one of two things: they either remind you to pray (which is fine, but doesn't help you actually do it), or they give you a feed of other people's prayer requests to read through (which is community, but not personal prayer).

What most free Christian prayer apps miss is the actual praying part.

I built the prayer features in FaithSpark to solve a specific problem I had: I knew I wanted to pray more, I just didn't know how to turn a vague intention into an actual daily practice. I needed something that would help me actually do it — not just remember to try.

## What to Look For in a Free Christian Prayer App

Before I walk through what FaithSpark does, here's the filter I'd use for any prayer app:

**Does it guide you through prayer or just remind you?** A reminder is a push notification. A guide actually walks you through what to pray. The first is better than nothing. The second is what actually builds a prayer life.

**Does it have a prayer list?** If you tell someone "I'll pray for you" and then forget by Thursday, you need a prayer list. A good prayer app saves names and requests and brings them back to you at regular intervals.

**Is it free?** Prayer shouldn't cost you a subscription. The core prayer features in a Christian app should be accessible to everyone.

**Does it feel like prayer?** Some apps feel clinical — forms to fill out, buttons to press. Prayer is relational. The app should feel like it's helping you talk to God, not complete a task.
